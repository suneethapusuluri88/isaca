ISACA's COBIT (Control Objectives for Information and Related Technologies) is a globally recognized framework for governance and management of enterprise information and technology (I&T). It helps organizations ensure that technology supports business goals, manages risk, optimizes resources, and delivers value.

What is COBIT?

COBIT provides a structured set of principles, processes, and best practices for governing and managing IT across an enterprise. It is used by:

IT managers
CIOs and CTOs
Internal and external auditors
Risk managers
Security professionals
Business executives

The framework bridges the gap between business objectives and IT operations.

Main Objectives

COBIT helps organizations:

Align IT strategy with business strategy
Deliver value from IT investments
Manage IT-related risks
Optimize resources (people, applications, infrastructure, data)
Measure and improve IT performance
Ensure compliance with laws and regulations
COBIT 2019

The latest major version is COBIT 2019, which is more flexible and customizable than earlier versions.

It introduces:

Governance and management objectives
Design factors for tailoring the governance system
Focus areas (such as cybersecurity, cloud, DevOps, and digital transformation)
Performance management using capability and maturity concepts
Core Components

A COBIT governance system consists of several components:

Processes
Activities that achieve governance and management objectives.
Organizational Structures
Decision-making bodies and reporting relationships.
Policies and Procedures
Rules that guide IT operations.
Information
Data required to support governance.
Culture, Ethics, and Behavior
Encourages responsible IT usage.
People, Skills, and Competencies
Ensures employees have appropriate capabilities.
Services, Infrastructure, and Applications
The technology assets supporting the business.
Governance vs. Management

COBIT distinguishes between governance and management:

Governance	Management
Evaluates stakeholder needs	Plans and executes activities
Sets direction	Implements strategy
Monitors performance	Operates day-to-day IT
